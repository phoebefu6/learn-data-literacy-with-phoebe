## Run of show

- 3-7 · Part 1 Drucker + Goodhart. Tell Wells Fargo slowly - "eight is great" gets a laugh, 3.5 million fake accounts kills it. Then the vanity trap: Color Labs, downloads up, activation near zero. Land the test: "what would we do differently if this number were half?"

- 11-18 · Part 3 the tree. THE core 7 minutes. Walk the SVG top down: income = revenue - cost; revenue = buyers x basket x item value; buyers = new + returning + resurrected - churned. Then the diagnosis walk: revenue down 8% → cost flat → buyers down → returning down. Punchline: "the tree decides who gets the Monday call - and gives three teams their morning back." Amazon WBR: inputs reviewed weekly, anomalies get an owner before they get an excuse.
- 18-28 · Apply-along 1 kpitree widget. Read the baseline out loud together (1,000 buyers, $75k revenue, $23k income). Challenge 1: predict BEFORE sliding churn to 400 - collect two guesses out loud first. Challenge 2: basket 3 → 3.5, compare. Challenge 3 recovery race in pairs: cheapest path back to $23k. Debrief line: "a 10% churn change beat a 10% price change - that is why retention teams exist."

- 34-39 · Apply-along 3 vanity audit. Quiet and slightly uncomfortable - that is fine. Harvest one proposed metric swap, applaud it.

## Expected questions
- "Our business does not have baskets - does the tree apply?" - Yes: any metric = its arithmetic parts. SaaS: ARR = accounts x seats x price per seat. B2B: revenue = deals x win rate x deal size. The multiplication is always hiding in there.
- "Who should own the tree itself?" - Finance or the analytics lead maintains it; each LEAF has one business owner. The tree is shared, the leaves are not.
- "Is one North Star not too simplistic for a complex business?" - It is a communication device, not a control system. Amazon runs hundreds of inputs; they still know what the flywheel serves. Start with one star, add guardrails, grow into a tree.
- "What about OKRs - do we need both?" - KPIs are the dashboard (health), OKRs are the quarterly bet (change). The tree tells you which branch deserves the next OKR.
- "How deep should the tree go?" - Stop at the layer where each leaf has exactly one owner and a data source. Deeper than that is decoration.
