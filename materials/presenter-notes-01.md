# Presenter notes - Session 1 · Think in data

## Preflight (5 min before)
- Open courses/01-think-in-data.html, click Projector zoom to 125%.
- Test the sorter widget once (Apply-along 2): click one right, one wrong - confirm shake + green.
- Have your own "data inventory" example ready (5 assets from Centience or a past company).
- Whiteboard marker for the ladder drawing fallback.

## Run of show
- 0-3 · Welcome. One line each: name, role, "one dashboard you saw this week". Sets the no-code promise out loud.
- 3-8 · Part 1 data types. Lead with the cost angle: structure = price tag on every question. Moneyball story from the card, 60 seconds max.
- 8-13 · Part 2 the ladder. Draw it on the whiteboard even though the SVG is on screen - the act of climbing sells it. Land the exec question: "what does this change?"
- 13-18 · Part 3 lifecycle. Hit the three failure points hard (step 1, 3, 6). The 60-80% cleaning stat gets an audible reaction - pause for it.
- 18-24 · Apply-along 1 data inventory. Solo 2 min, pairs 2 min, harvest 2 min. Push for the "most valuable, least used" asset.
- 24-32 · Apply-along 2 sorter widget. Everyone on their own device or one volunteer drives on screen. Statement 4 (rollback action) causes the best debate - let it run 2 min.
- 32-40 · Apply-along 3 decision autopsy. This one is quiet and personal - do not rush the writing minute. Harvest 2-3 "one sentence I will say" lines.
- 40-45 · Q&A + quiz. Quiz solo on devices, then show of hands per question.

## Never cut
- The "what does this chart change?" exec question - it is the course's spine.
- The 60-80% cleaning stat - it recalibrates every timeline conversation they will ever have.
- Apply-along 3 step 4 (the one sentence) - the take-home behavior.

## Cut if long
- Part 1 measurement levels goes to self-study pointer only (say "the table is on the page").
- Apply-along 1 pairs step - go straight from solo to harvest.
- The Target self-study story - point at the card instead.

## Expected questions
- "Is this course going to teach us SQL/Python?" - No. Literacy, not tooling. Your analysts keep their jobs.
- "What tool should we buy?" - Session 2 answers where data should live; tools come after questions, never before.
- "Our data is a mess, does any of this apply?" - Yes: messy data is WHY step 3 eats 60-80%. Session 2 shows what fixing the plumbing buys.
- "Where is AI in all this?" - Sessions 6-7 cover the ML that runs today's businesses; deep learning and GenAI get their own course later.
