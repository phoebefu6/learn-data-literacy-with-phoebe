## Run of show
- 0-3 · Welcome. Homework harvest from Session 4: "who found a branch nobody owns?" One or two stories. Frame today: "Session 4 built the tree; today you get the three x-ray machines behind its branches."
- 3-8 · Part 1 funnels. Lead with "every business is a funnel." Land the leak-first principle hard: weakest stage, not loudest stage. Baymard ~70% cart abandonment + shipping-cost-shown-late gets winces of recognition - pause for them. AARRR: one pass through the letters, promise the card has the rest.
- 8-13 · Part 2 cohorts. The magician's cape line: fast growth hides churn because new users answer for old ones. Trace both SVG curves with your hand: flattens = habit, dives = leaky bucket. Facebook "7 friends in 10 days" - one cohort insight became the whole onboarding strategy. Teach the five magic words: "show me this by cohort."
- 13-18 · Part 3 RFM. Three columns they already have: last purchase, count, spend. Walk the segment table; spend extra time on at-risk champions - recently valuable, most winnable, first in the win-back queue. Punchline: the whole point is to stop sending the same email to everyone.
- 18-27 · Apply-along 1 funnel widget. Read the baseline, find the flagged weakest stage. Then the boardroom duel: CMO doubles ad spend (visitors 200,000) vs product lead fixes add-to-cart +10 points. Run both live, write both revenue numbers on the whiteboard, ask "which was cheaper?" Debrief: leaks compound, upstream fixes multiply downstream - fix the funnel, THEN buy the traffic.
- 27-35 · Apply-along 2 rfm widget. Score the three customers together: friend of the brand (champion), big spender gone quiet a year (the at-risk profile), last-week newcomer. The discussion question does the work: "which segment does your marketing actually talk to?" Let the "everyone gets the same blast" confession land - it always comes.
- 35-40 · Apply-along 3 draw your funnel. Solo sketch, guessed conversions, mark the unowned stage. Harvest: "whose funnel has an orphan stage?" - usually a department handoff.
- 40-45 · Q&A + quiz. Quiz solo, show of hands. Q2 (growth vs retention) is the cohort lens paying off - say so.

## Expected questions
- "We are B2B with 40 customers - do funnels and RFM still apply?" - Funnels yes (lead → demo → proposal → close); RFM becomes account health scoring at that scale - same three questions, human judgment instead of quintiles.
- "What is a good retention number?" - Wrong first question: check the SHAPE first (flattening beats diving at any level), then benchmark within your industry pack, not across industries.
- "How is this different from what our CRM tool already does?" - The tool automates the scoring; this session is so YOU can interrogate what it recommends. Literacy first, license second.
- "Our funnel improved after the redesign - are we done?" - Was the sample big enough and the comparison fair? That is Session 3's A/B intuition - apply it before declaring victory.
- "Recency, frequency and monetary - which weighs most?" - For win-back, recency; for reward programs, monetary. The weights follow the decision, which is why we score them separately instead of averaging them into one number.
