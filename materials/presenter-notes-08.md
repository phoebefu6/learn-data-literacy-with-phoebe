# Presenter notes - Session 8 · From dashboard to decision

## Preflight (5 min before)
- Open courses/08-dashboard-to-decision.html, click Projector zoom to 125%.
- Run the kpitree widget once with the case numbers: churned 200 -> 400, new 400 -> 350, confirm income reads $4,250.
- Run the funnel widget once: restore add-to-cart 17 -> 30, confirm the recovered-revenue readout appears.
- Have a blank one-page memo template ready to hand out or screen-share for Apply-along 3.

## Run of show
- 0-3 · Welcome. Frame it: "Today there is nothing new - today we use everything." One line each: which session's tool they have already used at work.
- 3-9 · Part 1 diagnosis drill. Tell the Alpine Roast story cold: income $23,000 -> $4,250, marketing flat. Pause on the shock, then walk the tree on the SVG. Land: two quiet branch moves, two named problems, two owners.
- 9-14 · Part 2 drill kit. One tool per red branch: cohorts time it, funnel finds the mechanism, RFM ranks the rescue, forecast prices inaction ($0 in two quarters). Keep each finding to its one line.
- 14-19 · Part 3 memo + review hygiene. Read the Alpine Roast memo table out loud, answer-first. Amazon WBR gets 60 seconds; Domino's 30 as culture framing.
- 19-28 · Apply-along 1 kpitree. Everyone reproduces the collapse first (confirm $4,250 in the room), then the two recovery routes. The debrief line: fixing the leak beats compensating for it.
- 28-36 · Apply-along 2 funnel. Let them hunt for the leak before pointing at add-to-cart. The traffic-doubling comparison lands the "stop buying traffic into a leaking funnel" lesson.
- 36-41 · Apply-along 3 memo. Quiet writing time - do not talk over it. One volunteer reads theirs answer-first; applaud the first sentence specifically.
- 41-45 · Q&A + quiz + course close. End on the homework: run the loop on YOUR business this month.

## Never cut
- The $23,000 -> $4,250 reveal - the whole session hangs on that shock landing before the tree resolves it.
- The memo template (1 answer + 3 findings + 3 actions + 1 measurement line) - it is the course's take-home artifact.
- Apply-along 1 step 2 (recovery WITHOUT touching churn) - feeling how expensive compensation is teaches more than any slide.

## Cut if long
- The Domino's card - point at the page instead.
- Apply-along 2 step 3 (doubling visitors) - state the comparison verbally.
- The self-study boundary card - one sentence: "deep learning, recsys, search and GenAI are the next course."

## Expected questions
- "When do we get the AI course?" - It is the planned sibling course (AI literacy: deep learning, recommendations, search, GenAI). Everything from today transfers - same ladder, same memo, same re-read habit.
- "Our KPI tree is more complicated than this." - Good: build it once with your analyst, then this exact drill works on it. The arithmetic shape is the same.
- "Who should run the weekly metric review?" - The most senior person who will actually show up every week. Consistency beats seniority.
- "What if the tree shows everything moved a little?" - Then it is usually a market or seasonality story, not a branch story - that is a real diagnosis too, and the forecast tool is your next stop.
